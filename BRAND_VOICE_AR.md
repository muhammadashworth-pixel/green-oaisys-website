# Green Oaisys — Arabic Voice & Comms Identity (اللهجة البيضاء)

This is the **single source of truth** for all Green Oaisys Arabic — website, WhatsApp, email,
social, ads, decks, replies. Write Arabic **first and native**, never as a translation of English.
Every new Arabic string must pass this guide.

---

## 1. The voice in one line
**Clean "white" Arabic (اللهجة البيضاء): casual, warm, and confident — the way modern Saudi
startups talk (Salla, Tamara, Jahez, STC Pay, Foodics).** Spoken, not stiff. Professional, not slang.

We talk to a busy Saudi business owner like a sharp, trusted partner — simple words, short
sentences, real talk. Never corporate, never robotic, never heavy فصحى.

## 2. Principles
1. **Arabic-first.** Think the idea in Arabic and say it naturally. Don't translate word-for-word.
2. **White dialect, not heavy slang.** Understandable across the Gulf; light Saudi flavor. Avoid
   narrow local slang that a non-Najdi reader would stumble on.
3. **Spoken over formal.** Say it like you'd say it in a meeting, cleaned up.
4. **Short and direct.** One idea per sentence. Cut every word you can.
5. **Confident, warm, never salesy or hyped.** No exclamation spam, no empty superlatives.
6. **Respectful and clean.** No crude slang, no talking down to the owner.

## 3. Word choices — use ✅ / avoid ❌
| Meaning | ✅ White dialect | ❌ Too formal / off |
|---|---|---|
| that/which | اللي | التي / الذي / الذين |
| not | مو | ليس |
| what | وش | ماذا |
| so that | عشان | لكي / كي |
| you have | عندك | لديك |
| before (verb) | قبل ما | قبل أن |
| without (verb) | بدون ما | دون أن |
| let's / let us | خلّنا / خلّك | دعنا |
| see / look | شوف | انظر / اطّلع |
| choose | اختار | اختر |
| tomorrow | بكرة | غدًا |
| money | فلوس | (أموال is fine in formal spots; prefer فلوس) |
| work (noun) | الشغل | العمل (ok, but الشغل is warmer) |
| runs / works | يمشي / يشتغل | يعمل / يُدار |
| inside | جوّه / داخل | — |
| we want / you want | نبي / تبي | نريد / تريد |

Also: prefer **active voice** over MSA passive (يُدار، يُلاحَق، يُنفّذ → نديره، نلاحقه، ننفّذه).

## 4. Do / Don't
**Do**
- "نعرف وين يتسرب منك الوقت والفلوس."
- "فريقك يمسك الشغل اليومي وأنت تمسك الاتجاه."
- "احجز مكالمتك المجانية — 30 دقيقة وبس."
- "ما نبيع برامج. نبني طريقة شغل منشأتك."

**Don't**
- "نقوم بتحديد المواضع التي يتسرب منها الوقت والمال." (stiff, passive, MSA)
- "علاوةً على ذلك، يجدر بكم…" (corporate)
- Overhyped: "الأفضل على الإطلاق!!" — we don't shout.

## 5. CTAs (keep them spoken + specific)
- احجز مكالمتك المجانية ←
- قدّم على فحص منشأتك المجاني
- ابدأ الحين / خلّنا نبدأ
- تواصل معنا على واتساب

## 6. Mechanics
- **Numbers:** use Arabic-Indic or Western digits consistently within a string; prefer Western
  digits for figures like "30 دقيقة", "90 يوم", "12 سؤال" (matches how startups write).
- **English/tech terms:** keeping familiar ones is fine and natural — واتساب، CRM، AI،
  Calendly، Business X-Ray. Don't force awkward Arabic equivalents.
- **Emoji:** rare and purposeful (e.g., a single 🙏 or 🤔 in the chat mockups). Not in headings/CTAs.
- **Punctuation:** use the em dash — sparingly, like in English, for a beat.
- **Brand name:** غرين أويسِس.
- **RTL:** every Arabic string renders under `dir="rtl"`; keep Latin terms inline (they flip correctly).

## 7. Audience & register
Owner of a Saudi-based SME (3–30 staff) or a founder just starting out. Smart, time-poor, allergic
to fluff. Speak peer-to-peer: we already understand how their business runs.

## 8. Scope
Applies to **all** Arabic Green Oaisys produces. When adding any new Arabic string to the site
(`js/main.js` `I18N.ar`) or writing any customer message, match this guide and re-read it once
before shipping ("triple-check every word").
